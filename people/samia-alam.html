About

The Journey

Samia's path into computing started where most don't — not with high-level applications or web stacks, but with the question of what actually runs beneath all of it. That curiosity pulled her toward C, then Assembly, and eventually to the point where writing a program meant knowing exactly what the processor was doing at each step.

RISC-V became a natural draw. A clean, open ISA with a growing ecosystem made it the perfect playground for understanding processor architecture without the historical baggage of older instruction sets. Exploring it led to GNU Toolchain internals, linker scripts, and eventually SystemVerilog — because once you're curious about hardware, RTL is the next logical step.

Her work lives in personal projects and open-source repositories. Makefiles that actually make sense. Scripts that handle the boring parts cleanly. Assembly routines written to understand, not just to function. Each project is a deliberate attempt to own the full stack from source code down to silicon behavior.

The hobbyist label is worn with pride. Learning for its own sake, sharing what's discovered, and building things that are interesting — that's what drives the work. No agenda beyond genuine curiosity and the satisfaction of understanding something deeply.
