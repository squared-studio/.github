About

Background

Born and raised in Dhaka, Bangladesh, Mohiuddin Reyad was drawn early to electronics and digital systems. The idea that billions of MOSFETs, each only switching on and off, could combine inside a single chip to create extraordinary computational capability became a defining point of interest.

In 2022, he completed a Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from American International University Bangladesh. A university Verilog course provided the first practical entry point into hardware description languages and turned that interest into a concrete engineering path.

During the final semester, he accepted a trainee position at a VLSI engineering firm in Bangladesh. Over six months of intensive training under experienced professionals, he built core verification fundamentals while simultaneously contributing to work for a US-based client. That combination of learning and delivery shaped the way he approaches projects today.

After training, he moved into a junior design verification role and expanded into AXI4 custom IP verification using both class-based SystemVerilog and UVM. He also developed a testbench for a RISC-V floating point unit using Python cocotb, which deepened his appreciation for choosing the right verification style for the problem at hand.

As his responsibilities grew, design integration became part of the work as well, adding a wider systems view to protocol and arithmetic verification. The through-line has stayed consistent: build strong environments, catch edge cases early, and hand off verified RTL with confidence.

Today, that same mindset drives his work on demanding verification problems where methodical execution, practical debugging, and steady coverage closure matter more than presentation.
