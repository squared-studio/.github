Personal Story

The Journey

Amit's path into chip verification started with a fascination for digital systems and the precision required to prove that complex hardware behaves as intended. That curiosity developed into a career built around one of the most demanding corners of VLSI engineering: functional verification of high-speed protocols and processor cores.

He began his professional path as a Design Verification Engineer at SILICONOVA LIMITED in November 2022, where he built a solid foundation in UVM-based environments and learned how constrained-random stimulus, functional coverage, and layered testbench architecture come together to catch real bugs in real designs. What pulled him deeper into the field was the debugging side: understanding why hardware fails and tracing anomalies through LTSSM state machines, reset sequences, and SerDes layers to find the root cause.

A defining chapter has been PCIe Gen5 subsystem verification. Getting a high-speed serial link from training through stable enumeration and transaction-level operation demands disciplined verification and a deep protocol understanding that now carries into every subsequent project.

Alongside PCIe, he has worked on AXI MEM-SS, DMA controllers, NoC fabrics, and peripheral IPs, building and enhancing UVM testbenches, closing coverage with IMC, and identifying corner-case RTL bugs that constrained-random alone would not reliably find. He has also developed assembly-driven verification flows for RISC-V cores using golden reference models, which requires precise understanding of both the ISA and the implementation.

In August 2025, he joined TECKNOZ LIMITED as a Verification Engineer, continuing that trajectory on more demanding subsystem verification work. He remains motivated by the same thing that drew him into this field in the first place: the confidence that comes from thorough verification and the knowledge that the IP handed off has been genuinely exercised and is ready for silicon.
